Privacy Policy

Pact (Chrome extension) · Last updated June 2026

The short version: Pact does not collect, transmit, sell, or share your data — ever. There are no servers and no accounts. Everything Pact remembers stays on your own device.

What Pact stores

Pact saves the following locally in your browser (via chrome.storage.local), and nowhere else:

  • the list of websites you choose to block;
  • your settings (whether blocking is on, your Strict Mode commitment);
  • a local counter of blocked attempts and a daily streak.

This information never leaves your device. We cannot see it, and there is no account to sync it to.

What Pact does not do

  • No analytics, telemetry, or tracking.
  • No collection of your browsing history. Pact does not read, log, or transmit the pages you visit.
  • No external servers. No advertising. Nothing is ever sold or shared.

Permissions, and why

  • Storage — to save your blocklist and settings on your device.
  • declarativeNetRequest and host access — to redirect the sites you chose to block to Pact's own "Not now." page. This is used only to apply your own rules; Pact does not inspect or record your browsing.

Children

Pact is a general-audience productivity tool and is not directed at children under 13.

Honest note

Pact is a commitment device, not a lock. You can remove it at any time from your browser's extensions page. We never claim otherwise.
